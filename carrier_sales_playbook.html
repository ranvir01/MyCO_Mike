Section 3

Pre-Visit Preparation

Materials Checklist

Before leaving for store visits, ensure you have:

Essential Materials

  • Flyers: 5-10 per store, plus extras (print on quality cardstock)
  • Business cards: Mike's AND your own
  • Referral cards: 20+ per store
  • Partnership agreements: 3-5 printed copies
  • Calculator loaded: Trade-in calculator open on phone, ready to demo
  • Notepad & pen: For contact info and notes

Professional Appearance

  • Dress code: Business casual minimum
  • Clean appearance: Hair, clothes, shoes presentable
  • Confident posture: Stand tall, make eye contact
  • Friendly demeanor: Smile, be approachable

Best Times to Visit

DO Visit:

  • Tuesday - Thursday, between 10am-11am or 2pm-4pm
  • After the morning rush, before the afternoon picks up
  • Mid-week when managers are more likely to be present

DON'T Visit:

  • Monday mornings (busiest, staff catching up)
  • Saturday afternoons (peak customer time)
  • Lunch rush (12pm-1pm, staff is busy or on break)
  • Last hour before closing (staff wrapping up)

Mindset Before Walking In

Your Attitude

"I'm here to help you close more sales. That's it."

You're not selling something they don't need. You're offering a solution to a problem they already have. Every store loses sales because of cracked screens. You're offering to fix that.

Key Reminders

  1. You have value to offer — This isn't cold calling, it's problem-solving
  2. They can say no — And that's okay. Move on to the next
  3. You're building relationships — Even a "no" today can be a "yes" later
  4. Be genuinely helpful — Don't be pushy, be useful
Section 4

The 5-Minute Pitch Script

Step 1: The Approach (30 seconds)

Walk in confidently. Look for the manager or most senior employee on the floor. Make eye contact and smile.

Say This:
"Hi there! Is the manager available? I'm [YOUR NAME] from MyCo Consulting — I work with Gadget Fix, the phone repair shop just down the road.

I've got a quick idea that could help you close more upgrade sales. Do you have 3 minutes?"
If Manager Isn't Available:
"No problem! When's a good time to catch them? I've got something that helps stores close more sales."

Get their name and best time. Leave a flyer. Note it down. Move on.

Step 2: The Hook (30 seconds)

Once you have their attention, identify the problem they already know exists.

Say This:
"Quick question — how often do customers come in wanting to trade in their phone for an upgrade, but the screen is cracked?"

[Pause — let them answer]

"Right? And you either have to turn them away or offer a much lower trade-in value. Frustrating for everyone — especially when they walk out without buying."

Step 3: The Solution (60 seconds)

Now present the solution. Pull out your phone with the calculator ready.

Say This:
"What if you could send those customers to get their screen fixed in 15-20 minutes, and they come back ready for full trade-in value?

Gadget Fix is literally [X] minutes from here. They specialize in iPhone screen repairs. Most are done while the customer waits.

Here's the cool part..."

[Show calculator on phone — walk through an example]

"Let's say someone comes in with a cracked iPhone 16. Right now they'd get maybe $180 on trade-in. But watch this:

• They pay $139 for the screen repair
• They come back, trade in for $400
• That's $81 extra in their pocket — AFTER paying for the repair!

You get to complete the sale. They get a better deal. Mike at Gadget Fix gets a customer. Everyone wins."

Step 4: The Ask (60 seconds)

Make it easy to say yes. No big commitment.

Say This:
"I'm not asking for anything complicated. Just:

1. Keep a few of our flyers at the counter or in the break room
2. When a customer has a cracked screen affecting their trade-in, mention Gadget Fix
3. That's it.

We can also set up a referral bonus — $5 to $10 for each customer you send who completes a repair. Totally optional, whatever works for you.

Can I leave some flyers with you?"

Step 5: The Close (60 seconds)

If They Say YES:
"Awesome! Here are some flyers. And here's Mike's card if anyone has questions — he's the owner, super responsive.

I'll check back in a couple weeks to see how it's going and if you need more materials.

What's the best way to reach you? Email or text?"

[Write down their contact info]

"Thanks so much! Looking forward to a great partnership."
If They Need to "Check With Someone":
"Totally understand. Here's info you can share with your manager. Can I get your email to follow up in a few days?"

Section 5

Objection Handling Guide

Common Objections & Word-for-Word Responses

"We already have a repair partner."
"That's great! We're not looking to replace anyone. We just want to be an additional option for your customers — specifically for trade-in situations. Think of it as giving your customers more choices. Can we be a backup option?"
"I need to check with my manager / corporate."
"Totally understand — chain of command matters. Here's some info you can share with them. It's really just about keeping some flyers available, nothing official.

What's the best way for me to follow up? I can send an email with all the details."
"We don't do referrals."
"No problem at all — the referral bonus is completely optional. Even just having our flyers available when a customer has a cracked screen helps. It's really about giving your customers a solution and helping you close more sales. Can I leave some flyers?"
"Customers won't want to leave and come back."
"That's a fair point. But here's what we've found: most repairs take just 15-20 minutes. Some customers browse around while they wait. And when they realize they'll get an extra $80-150 on their trade-in? That 20-minute trip becomes totally worth it.

Plus, they come back happier and ready to buy."
"What's in it for us?"
"Great question. Three things:

1. You close more upgrade sales. Instead of losing that customer, they come back ready to buy.
2. Happier customers. They feel like you helped them get a better deal.
3. Optional bonus. If you want, we can do $5-10 per referral who completes a repair.

But really, it's about not losing those upgrade sales."
"How do I know you're legit?"
"Great question — you should ask that! Here's our website... [show on phone]

We offer a 90-day warranty on all repairs. Mike, the owner, has been in phone repair for years and has great Google reviews. Feel free to call him directly — he's happy to answer any questions.

We're just a small local business trying to help other local businesses."
"We're really busy right now."
"I totally get it — I'll be quick. Can I just leave some flyers and my card? I'll swing by another time when it's calmer. When's usually a slower time for you?"
"The carrier has policies against this."
"I understand — you have to follow corporate guidelines. We're not asking for an official partnership or to be endorsed. It's really just... if a customer asks what they can do about their cracked screen, you might mention there's a repair shop nearby. Totally informal.

But I get it if that doesn't work. Can I at least leave my card in case things change?"

When to Walk Away

Not every store will be a good fit. Walk away gracefully if:

  • They're clearly not interested and getting annoyed
  • They have a corporate policy that genuinely prohibits it
  • The manager is hostile or dismissive
What to Say:
"I totally understand. Thanks for your time! If things ever change, here's my card. Have a great day!"

Always leave on a positive note. Situations change, and you might be back.

Follow-Up & Relationship Building

Post-Visit Checklist

Complete this after EVERY store visit:

Immediately After the Visit

  • Note the store name, address, and date visited
  • Record the manager's name (even if not available)
  • Note the best time to return if follow-up needed
  • Log whether materials were left
  • Get contact info (email/phone) if possible
  • Note any objections or concerns raised
  • Rate the visit: Hot / Warm / Cold / Not Interested

Within 24 Hours

  • Enter visit details into tracking spreadsheet
  • Send follow-up email if promised
  • Text Mike with any hot leads
  • Schedule follow-up reminder in calendar
